# CreditOps Cover Prompt Design

## Status and scope

**CONFIRMED:** This specification defines the visual direction and image-generation prompt for a horizontal 16:9 CreditOps website or GitHub banner.

**CONFIRMED:** The cover communicates a verifiable, evidence-centered multi-agent system that supports preparation and review of SME working-capital credit cases. It must not imply autonomous credit approval, policy waiver, or operational authority.

## Confirmed creative direction

- **Audience:** banking leaders and banking specialists.
- **Reference style:** premium cinematic 3D fintech, minimal composition, floating objects, soft depth of field, and restrained luminous data connections.
- **Core concept:** Digital Twin Core.
- **Hero object:** a translucent three-dimensional credit-case dossier representing the Credit Case Digital Twin.
- **Supporting system:** six abstract specialist nodes connected to documents, facts, calculations, and findings through a luminous EvidenceGraph.
- **Control metaphor:** a transparent protective ring around the core, representing traceability, deterministic controls, and human-governed checkpoints. It must not resemble an approval stamp.
- **Palette:** warm muted orange, copper-orange metallic highlights, ivory, champagne beige, and soft white.
- **On-image text:** `CreditOps` only.
- **Branding:** no logo and no tagline.
- **Format:** horizontal 16:9 banner only.

## Composition

The Credit Case Digital Twin is the visual center of gravity. Its translucent dossier layers contain abstract document sheets, structured fields, evidence markers, and fine graph lines without readable customer data. Six restrained specialist nodes orbit the core at different depths. Their connections form a clear evidence network rather than decorative circuitry.

The transparent control ring encloses or intersects the core without using a checkmark, approval seal, credit score, or decision label. The background is an architectural ivory-beige 3D space with soft platforms, sparse particles, and generous negative space. The result should feel trustworthy, executive, and technologically advanced rather than futuristic or playful.

## Image-generation prompt

```text
Create a premium cinematic 3D horizontal website and GitHub banner for a banking technology project titled exactly “CreditOps”. Place a sophisticated translucent Credit Case Digital Twin dossier near the center-left, built from frosted glass, refined brushed metal, layered abstract documents, structured data fields, evidence markers, calculation traces, and fine provenance links. Do not show readable customer information.

Extend a precise luminous EvidenceGraph across the wide composition. Six elegant abstract specialist nodes orbit the dossier at different depths and connect source documents, facts, calculations, evidence markers, and review findings back to the central case. Keep the graph legible, sparse, and purposeful. Add a subtle transparent protective control ring around the core to communicate governance, deterministic safeguards, auditability, and human-controlled checkpoints; it must not resemble a credit approval stamp or autonomous decision system.

Use warm muted orange and copper-orange accents with ivory, champagne beige, frosted white glass, gentle amber edge lighting, realistic ray-traced reflections, soft shadows, subtle volumetric glow, restrained particles, shallow depth of field, and a minimal architectural 3D environment. Premium executive banking aesthetic, trustworthy and technologically advanced, refined cinematic product visualization, strong horizontal flow, generous negative space for the title.

Display only the word “CreditOps” in large clean modern sans-serif typography, correctly spelled, in the open center-right area. No logo, no tagline, no other readable text. Horizontal banner, 16:9 aspect ratio, ultra-detailed, high resolution.
```

## Production note

Image generators may misspell on-image typography. If exact lettering is critical, generate the composition with a clean title area and add `CreditOps` as a separate typographic layer afterward. This does not change the approved visual direction.
